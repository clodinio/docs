# Projects and Folders

WorkHub organizes your work into projects and folders. This structure keeps recordings and their completed transcripts together, supports team navigation, and scales across multi-phase studies and large-volume workflows.

## Creating a Project

1. Navigate to **WorkHub** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Project**
3. Give your project a name and confirm

Projects sit at the top level of your workspace. Each project can contain folders and individual files.

## Creating Folders Within a Project

Within a project you can create subfolders to organize recordings by phase, participant group, site, language, or any other dimension that suits your workflow.

1. Open a project
2. Click **New Folder**
3. Name the folder and confirm

## Navigating Your Workspace

Click any project or folder to open it and view its contents. Use the breadcrumb trail at the top of the screen to navigate back through the hierarchy.

## What a Project or Folder Can Contain

- Audio and video recordings awaiting transcription
- Completed transcripts returned to the workspace
- Both the recording and its transcript, kept together for reference

## Naming Conventions

Consistent naming makes a significant difference on large projects. Consider organizing by:

**By study or project:**

- `UCSF Diabetes Study 2026`
- `Consumer Insights Phase 2`

**By client or matter:**

- `Smith v. Jones Deposition Set`
- `Pfizer KOL Interviews Q2`

**By research phase:**

- `Recruitment Interviews`
- `Follow-up Sessions`
- `Focus Group Series A`

**By language or geography:**

- `Spanish IDIs Mexico`
- `French Focus Groups Paris`

<Tip>
  Set up your folder structure at the beginning of a project. Reorganizing a large archive of completed transcripts is significantly more time-consuming than building the structure before you begin uploading.
</Tip>
