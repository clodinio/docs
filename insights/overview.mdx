## The Instant Draft and Smart Insights Workflow

1. Upload your file and select **Instant Draft**
2. Your transcript is returned within minutes
3. Open it in Smart Insights to generate analysis outputs
4. Use the outputs as a starting point for your work

Smart Insights accelerates the point at which your analysis can begin. Its outputs are starting points to interrogate and build on, not finished findings.

## What Smart Insights Generates

**Summary**: A structured overview of the transcript covering the key findings, critical moments, and the single most important thing the participant communicated. Auto-generated when you open a transcript.

**Key Moments:** The most analytically significant moments in the transcript — turning points, unexpected revelations, strongest statements, emotional shifts, and tensions. Includes a standout statement index with timestamps and a single most important moment call-out.

**Interview Analysis:** A deep interpretive read of the interview across six dimensions: how the participant constructs their position, what they say and what they avoid, narrative arc, underlying assumptions, power and rapport dynamics, and an interpretive summary. The most analytically rich output Smart Insights produces.

**Themes:** A structured thematic analysis identifying recurring patterns, tensions, and narratives across the transcript. Not word frequency, a pattern analysis that surfaces the ideas running through the conversation, organised into a thematic map with supporting evidence.

**Research Synthesis:** A cross-cutting synthesis of the interview's core argument, internal logic, and contribution to the broader research question. Useful for multi-transcript projects where each interview needs a positioned summary before comparison.

**Q&A Pairs:** Every question and answer exchange structured and analyzed. Shows how the participant responded, what they engaged with directly, what they deflected, and where the most substantive exchanges occurred.

**Contradictions:** Identifies moments where the participant's account conflicts with itself, shifts position, or holds in tension with something they said elsewhere. Surfaces these for review, does not draw conclusions.

**Research Notes:** Auto-generated structured notes from the session formatted for team sharing or research archiving. A starting framework for your own analytical write-up, organised around key observations and analytical pointers.

**Legal Analysis**

**Witness Summary** A factual, topic-by-topic account of the witness's testimony. Gives counsel a structured record of what was said on each key issue without requiring a full read-through before preparation begins. No legal conclusions drawn.

**Event Timeline** A chronological reconstruction of events as described by the witness, exactly as stated, with gaps and conflicts noted. Useful for establishing sequence, identifying missing periods, and spotting internal timeline inconsistencies.

**Key Concessions** A structured log of statements where the witness acknowledged, confirmed, or conceded a fact. Includes qualified concessions and notes what each acknowledgement places on the record. No legal conclusions drawn.

**Non-Answers** A log of every question that was not directly answered, deflected, met with "I don't recall", or left incomplete. Categorised by response type. Includes a pattern summary across the testimony.

**Key Statements** A navigable index of the most evidentially significant statements in the transcript, with verbatim quotes, timestamps, and a quick-reference timestamp list grouped by subject matter.

**Contradiction Flags** Apparent inconsistencies within the witness's own testimony, where a statement conflicts with, qualifies, or contradicts something said earlier. Includes a separate section for position shifts and qualifications. Flagged for attorney review only, not evidence of untruthfulness.

**Coding CSV Export** A structured file formatted for direct import into NVivo, ATLAS.ti, MAXQDA, or Dedoose. Your thematic structure is pre-organized so you can begin coding immediately.

**Findings Deck Outline** A structured outline for turning your findings into a presentation or report.

**AI Chat Across Transcripts**: Ask questions directly across your transcript. Search for specific statements, compare responses, locate relevant testimony, and explore patterns across interviews. Available on Pro (up to 20 transcripts) and Advanced (unlimited).

## Who Uses Smart Insights

**Academic researchers and PhD candidates.** Preliminary thematic orientation before deep coding. Reduces the time between completing fieldwork and beginning substantive analysis.

**Market research and insights teams.** Rapid synthesis of IDI and focus group data. Theme summaries, quotes banks, and deck outlines accelerate the path from fieldwork to debrief.

**Legal teams.** Instant Draft on depositions, witness interviews, and hearing recordings followed by Smart Insights for fast orientation across a transcript set. AI Chat is particularly useful for locating specific testimony and statements across multiple transcripts quickly.

**UX researchers.** Fast first-pass analysis of usability sessions and user interview sets.

**Public health and fieldwork teams.** Early thematic scanning across large sets of field interviews, including multilingual research across all supported languages.

**Enterprise teams.** Rapid synthesis of stakeholder interviews, board meetings, earnings calls, and compliance recordings.

## Smart Insights and Human Transcription

Smart Insights is not available for Human Transcription outputs. This is intentional.

Clients who choose Human Transcription require human-only data handling throughout, many operate under IRB protocols, HIPAA requirements, legal confidentiality obligations, or institutional data governance policies that prohibit AI processing of their content. Smart Insights does not touch those transcripts at any stage.

A common workflow for teams that need both: use Instant Draft and Smart Insights for a fast preliminary pass, then commission Human Transcription for the recordings that will anchor final analysis, formal documentation, or legal proceedings.

## Security and Data Handling

Smart Insights processes transcript text using Anthropic Claude accessed via AWS Bedrock. For EU accounts, processing occurs within AWS Bedrock's EU infrastructure. Neither Anthropic nor AWS Bedrock retains or uses submitted content for model training.

Smart Insights processes transcript text only, not the original audio file. All data is handled under the same 30-day retention policy as the rest of the platform.
