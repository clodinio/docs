## What We Transcribe

**Patient interviews:** Patient-reported outcomes, lived experience narratives, treatment journey discussions, and side effect accounts transcribed with precision for regulatory and analytical use.

**HCP interviews.** Healthcare provider conversations including prescribing behavior research, KOL engagements, medical market research interviews, and clinical advisory sessions.

**Clinical trial discussions.** Investigator meetings, site visits, protocol discussions, safety reviews, and regulatory meeting records.

**Focus groups with healthcare participants.** Multi-speaker recordings from patient advisory panels, HCP focus groups, and payer research sessions.

**Patient-reported outcome sessions.** PRO recordings capturing quality-of-life impacts, symptom patterns, and patient experience narratives for clinical development purposes.

**MSL conversations.** Medical Science Liaison interactions with KOLs and HCPs, documenting scientific exchange for medical affairs teams.

**Advisory board sessions.** Expert panel recordings, steering committee discussions, and strategic advisory meetings.

**Behavioral health sessions.** Clinical interviews, therapy session recordings (with appropriate consent), and behavioral health research conversations handled with the sensitivity and confidentiality this content requires.

## HIPAA Compliance

Qualtranscribe maintains HIPAA-compliant workflows for all projects involving Protected Health Information. Key provisions:

- Business Associate Agreements signed before any PHI is submitted
- PHI stored exclusively in Wasabi's us-east-1 region (North Virginia, USA); data never leaves the United States
- 256-bit AES encryption at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit
- Access limited to the assigned transcriptionist and project manager, with full audit logging
- HIPAA training mandatory for all staff handling healthcare content
- Covered entity notified within 24 hours of any confirmed PHI breach

See [HIPAA Compliance](/account/hipaa) for the full compliance framework.

## AI Features for Healthcare Research

**Instant Draft:** For HIPAA-active accounts, Instant Draft audio is processed exclusively on AssemblyAI's US-hosted endpoints under a Business Associate Agreement. Audio is deleted upon transcript completion.

**Smart Insights:** Transcript analysis via Anthropic Claude on AWS Bedrock with SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001 certifications, covered by a Business Associate Agreement. Useful for synthesizing large sets of patient interviews, HCP interviews, or clinical discussion recordings.

Clients who require that no AI tools process their PHI should select Human Transcription only and not use these features.

## Clinical Terminology Accuracy

Medical transcription requires transcriptionists who understand the difference between hyperglycemia and hypoglycemia, who can correctly transcribe drug names like obinutuzumab, and who recognize when a participant is referencing a specific clinical endpoint. AI tools fail on exactly these cases. Qualtranscribe's transcriptionists handle clinical and pharmaceutical terminology, therapeutic area vocabulary, and research methodology language accurately.

For projects with highly specialized therapeutic area vocabulary (oncology, rare diseases, neurology, immunology), include a glossary in your order notes. We can also assign transcriptionists with relevant subject matter experience for complex projects.

## Common Questions

**Do you provide transcription for IRB-approved clinical studies?** Yes. We are familiar with IRB data management requirements and provide full compliance documentation for IRB submissions. See [IRB Compliance](/compliance/irb).

**Can you handle recordings from Zoom or Teams clinical sessions?** Yes. We support import directly from Zoom and Webex, as well as direct upload of Teams recordings (MP4 from OneDrive).

**What if the audio quality is poor?** We notify you within 24-48 hours if audio is unsuitable for transcription, and offer options including best-effort transcription with a 25% discount. For clinical documentation where a section is genuinely unintelligible, we mark it \[inaudible\] with a timecode rather than providing an inaccurate guess.
