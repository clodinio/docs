## Language Coverage

**Human Transcription (20\+ languages):**

Spanish, French, German, Italian, Portuguese, Arabic, Mandarin, Japanese, Swahili, Amharic, Turkish, Russian, Dutch, Polish, Norwegian, Ukrainian, Korean, Hindi, Greek, Romanian, and more. Language coverage is expanding; contact support if your language is not listed.

**Instant Draft AI Transcription (99\+ languages):**

All major world languages plus a broad range of additional languages including Tagalog, Vietnamese, Thai, Indonesian, Malay, Bengali, Hebrew, Czech, Hungarian, and many more. For lower-resource languages or recordings with heavy regional accents, accuracy varies; Human Transcription is recommended.

## Translation Services

**Bilingual direct transcription** is the most cost-effective approach when you only need the English output. A native bilingual transcriptionist listens to your source-language audio and transcribes directly into English, without producing an intermediate source-language transcript. Available at **\$6.00 per audio minute**.

For a 60-minute interview, this costs $360, compared to $1,050-1,500 using the traditional two-step approach. The savings are 65-76%.

**Two-step transcription and translation** produces both a source-language transcript and an English translation. Required when you need the original-language transcript for verification, academic citation, legal documentation, or bilingual reporting. Priced as separate transcription and translation fees.

## Regional Dialect Matching

Spanish is not one language. A recording from Mexico City requires different linguistic expertise than one from Buenos Aires, Bogotá, or Madrid. Portuguese from Brazil differs substantially from European Portuguese. Arabic spoken in Cairo sounds different from Moroccan Arabic.

We match transcriptionists to the specific dialect and regional variety of your recording. When placing your order, note the country or region where the recording was made so we can assign appropriately.

## Code-Switching and Spanglish

Bilingual speakers frequently switch between languages mid-sentence. This is common in US Latino communities, multilingual fieldwork environments, and international professional settings. Our transcriptionists handle code-switching accurately, preserving the original language patterns rather than normalizing to a single language.

## African Languages

We provide transcription and translation support for African languages including Swahili, Amharic, Yoruba, Hausa, Igbo, Twi, Wolof, Xhosa, Zulu, Shona, Somali, and more. Many of these are tonal languages where transcription errors can completely change meaning; native-speaker transcriptionists are essential.

For less commonly supported African languages, contact us before ordering to confirm availability.

## European Languages

We support all major European languages including Finnish, Hungarian, Polish, Romanian, Czech, Greek, Bulgarian, and others with complex grammar structures that automated tools struggle with. Native speakers are matched to the specific regional variety where relevant (e.g., Castilian vs. Latin American Spanish, Brazilian vs. European Portuguese).

## Multilingual Research Workflows

For research programs involving multiple languages simultaneously, we offer:

- Parallel transcription across languages with consistent speaker labeling conventions
- Consistent terminology across languages for cross-market analysis
- Rolling delivery by language group to support phased analysis
- Smart Insights analysis available in any language (output in English)
- Coding CSV exports compatible with NVivo and ATLAS.ti for multilingual data sets

## GDPR and Multilingual EU Research

For research involving EU participants in multiple languages, all data is processed within EU infrastructure. Transcriptionists outside the EEA operate under signed Data Processing Agreements that meet GDPR transfer requirements. See [GDPR Compliance](/compliance/gdpr).
