## Why Accuracy Matters in Legal Work

A single misheard phrase in a deposition transcript can change the meaning of testimony. AI transcription tools routinely misidentify technical terms, produce incorrect speaker attributions, and make confident errors on legal jargon that they have not been adequately trained on. An independent study by the CISPA Helmholtz Center confirmed that AI transcription consistently produces meaning-distorting errors on specialized vocabulary. Human transcription does not.

For legal work, every word is part of the record. Qualtranscribe uses human transcriptionists with legal terminology training for all legal proceedings.

## Legal Formatting Options

- **Full Verbatim:** Captures every utterance, pause, filler word, and non-verbal sound. Standard for depositions and formal legal documentation.
- **Clean Verbatim:** Removes filler words while preserving the complete substantive record. Used for advisory notes, client summaries, and internal documentation.
- **Speaker Labels:** Examination counsel, opposing counsel, witness, judge, and other participants clearly identified throughout.
- **Timestamps:** At every speaker turn or at regular intervals for audio cross-referencing.
- **Line numbering:** Available on request for deposition and court transcript formatting.

## Common Questions

**Is a legal transcriptionist the same as a court reporter?** No. A court reporter transcribes proceedings in real time, typically using stenography. A legal transcriptionist works from recorded audio or video after the fact, producing accurate written records for legal teams. Both serve important and distinct roles in the legal process.

**How long does it take?** Standard turnaround is 3-5 business days. Expedited and rush options are available. For files with poor audio quality or very high speaker density, contact us before ordering to discuss realistic timelines.

**Can you handle poor audio quality?** Our transcriptionists are trained to work with challenging audio including courtroom environments, phone recordings, and older analog sources. Sections that are genuinely unintelligible will be marked \[inaudible\] with a timecode rather than guessed at.

**Do you work with case management systems?** Transcripts can be exported in DOCX, PDF, plain text, and SRT formats for import into most case management and e-discovery platforms. Contact support to discuss specific integration requirements.
