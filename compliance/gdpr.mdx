# GDPR Compliance

Qualtranscribe is fully compliant with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR, EU 2016/679). This page covers our data protection practices, your rights as a data subject or data controller, how we handle EU participant data, and how to request a Data Processing Agreement for your organization.

## Our Role Under GDPR

Qualtranscribe acts as a **data processor** on behalf of its clients. Our clients, as the organizations directing research, healthcare workflows, or legal proceedings, act as **data controllers**. This means:

- You determine the purposes and means of processing
- We process personal data only on your instructions and within the scope required to deliver transcription and related services
- We do not use your data for any purpose beyond what you have authorized

## Lawful Basis for Processing

We process personal data under the following lawful bases:

- **Contractual necessity:** Processing required to deliver the transcription or translation services you have ordered
- **Legitimate interests:** Operational needs that do not override your rights (e.g., quality assurance)
- **Legal obligations:** Compliance with applicable laws and regulations
- **Consent:** Where you have provided explicit consent for specific processing activities

## EU Data Residency

EU client data is stored exclusively within the EEA. Specifically:

- **File storage:** Wasabi Technologies, eu-central-2 region (Frankfurt, Germany)
- **Instant Draft AI processing:** AssemblyAI's EU infrastructure, ensuring audio never leaves the EEA
- **Smart Insights AI processing:** Anthropic Claude via AWS Bedrock EU infrastructure, ensuring transcript content stays within the EEA
- **Application and account data:** Supabase, under a data processing agreement

Your data residency region is set at account creation. EU accounts never process data on US infrastructure.

## What Personal Data We Process

We process only the data necessary to deliver your requested service:

- Audio and video files submitted for transcription, which may contain identifiable information about participants
- Completed transcript text
- Contact and billing information for your account
- File metadata (upload timestamps, file names, sizes)

We do not process data for advertising, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to service delivery.

## No AI Training on Your Data

Neither Qualtranscribe nor any of our subprocessors (AssemblyAI, Anthropic/AWS Bedrock) uses client data to train AI models. This applies to all accounts including the Free tier. Your participant recordings and transcripts remain yours and are never repurposed.

## Data Retention

File retention is configurable from account settings under **HIPAA & Compliance**, with options from 30 days (default) through 60, 90, 180 days, 1 year, or never (manual deletion). Earlier deletion is available on request and is processed within five business days. Account-level contact and billing information is retained for the period required by applicable tax and legal regulations (typically 7 years). Communication records are retained for 1 year.

Upon deletion, data is removed from active systems and backups, overwritten using industry-standard methods, and verified as completely erased. A Certificate of Destruction is available upon request.

## Your Rights as a Data Subject

Under GDPR, you and your research participants have the following rights:

- **Right of access:** Obtain confirmation of whether we hold your personal data and receive a copy
- **Right to rectification:** Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
- **Right to erasure:** Request deletion of your personal data subject to legal retention requirements
- **Right to restriction:** Request that we restrict processing in certain circumstances
- **Right to data portability:** Receive your data in a structured, commonly used format
- **Right to object:** Object to processing based on legitimate interests
- **Right to withdraw consent:** Withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing

To exercise any of these rights: email support@qualtranscribe.com with "GDPR Request" in the subject line. We may verify your identity before processing certain requests.

## Data Processing Agreements

A signed Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is available for any client who requires one. DPAs outline our data handling responsibilities, security measures, subprocessor arrangements, and obligations in the event of a breach.

To request a DPA: contact support@qualtranscribe.com.

## Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, Qualtranscribe will:

- Notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach
- Notify affected clients without undue delay
- Provide details of the breach, categories of data affected, and remediation steps taken

## GDPR and Research Workflows

For researchers working with EU participants or institutions:

**Consent and transparency:** Your participant consent forms should disclose that recordings will be transcribed by a professional service operating under GDPR-compliant data handling. We can provide documentation of our data processing practices for inclusion in your ethics submissions.

**Data minimization:** Where possible, minimize the personal identifiers present in recordings before submission. We also offer anonymization and redaction services for transcripts.

**Participant data requests:** If a research participant exercises their right of erasure and you need us to delete their recording and transcript, contact support@qualtranscribe.com. We will process deletion requests within five business days.

**DPAs for institutional research:** Many universities and research institutions require a signed DPA between the researcher and any third-party processor. We provide DPAs on request.
