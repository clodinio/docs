title: "Import from Zoom"
description: "You can import Zoom cloud recordings directly into Qualtranscribe without downloading them to your device."
---

## Before You Import

Zoom recordings must be saved to **Zoom Cloud** (not local) to be importable. Cloud recordings appear in your Zoom web portal at zoom.us usually within 5-30 minutes after your meeting ends, though longer recordings can take up to an hour to process.

If you recorded locally, locate your recording in your computer's Documents/Zoom folder and upload it as a standard device upload.

## Connecting Zoom

1. From the upload panel, click **Zoom**
2. Sign in to your Zoom account when prompted
3. Grant Qualtranscribe access to your cloud recordings
4. Your Zoom recordings will appear in the file picker

## Selecting a Recording

Browse your cloud recordings and select the one you want to transcribe. Zoom recordings are typically in MP4 (video) or M4A (audio-only) format, both of which are fully supported.

## Recording Quality

Zoom recording quality depends on the bandwidth available during the meeting. If participants had poor connections, the recording reflects that. For research interviews and focus groups conducted via Zoom, we recommend:

- Asking all participants to join via a wired connection where possible
- Requesting that participants use headphones to reduce echo
- Enabling separate audio tracks in Zoom settings, which records each participant's audio independently and improves transcription accuracy for multi-speaker sessions

<Note>
  Zoom's built-in transcription has significant accuracy limitations, particularly for technical terminology, accents, and multi-speaker recordings. Qualtranscribe human transcription delivers substantially higher accuracy for research and professional use.
</Note>
