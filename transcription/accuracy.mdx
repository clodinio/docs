## The 99%\+ Standard

Qualtranscribe maintains a 99%\+ accuracy standard for clearly recorded audio. "Clearly recorded" means:

- Speakers are distinguishable from one another
- Background noise does not obscure speech
- Recording volume is adequate
- Speech is clear (not excessively fast, slurred, or heavily accented beyond transcriptionist capability)

## Factors That Affect Accuracy

| Factor | Impact |
| --- | --- |
| Background noise (moderate) | Medium |
| Background noise (heavy) | High |
| Overlapping speakers (moderate crosstalk) | Medium |
| Overlapping speakers (heavy simultaneous speech) | High |
| Strong regional accents | Medium to High |
| Domain-specific terminology without glossary | Medium |
| Low recording volume | Medium |
| Phone-quality or heavily compressed audio | Medium |
| Recording older than 10 years (tape, analog) | Variable |

## Human Transcription vs. AI Accuracy

Human transcriptionists and AI tools perform very differently across content types:

| Content Type | Human Accuracy | AI Accuracy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Clear single speaker, standard vocabulary | 99%\+ | 90-95% |
| Academic research interviews | 99%\+ | 85-90% |
| Focus groups (3-8 speakers) | 98-99% | 85-90% |
| Technical or medical content | 99%\+ | 85-90% |
| Legal depositions | 99%\+ | 85-95% |
| Accented English | 98-99% | 85-90% |
| Background noise present | 98-99% | 80-90% |

For any project where accuracy has professional, legal, or ethical consequences, Human Transcription is the appropriate service.

## How to Improve Your Results

**Before recording:**

- Test your equipment and room acoustics before your actual session
- Choose a quiet room; soft furnishings reduce echo
- Use an external or lapel microphone where possible
- For multi-participant sessions, use boundary or directional microphones

**During recording:**

- Ask participants to speak one at a time
- Have everyone introduce themselves at the start
- Avoid recording near fans, air conditioning units, or open windows

**When placing your order:**

- Use the order notes field to provide speaker names, technical terminology, subject matter context, and any known audio challenges
- Include a glossary for specialized vocabulary (drug names, legal terms, research-specific concepts)
- Flag known audio problems upfront so your transcriptionist can prepare

## Flagging Unclear Sections

Where speech is genuinely unintelligible, your transcriptionist will insert a notation such as \[inaudible 00:23:45\] rather than guessing. This preserves the integrity of your data and allows you to listen to the original recording for those sections.

If you know what was said in a flagged section and would like the transcript updated, submit a revision request with the corrected text and the corresponding timecode.

## If Your Transcript Falls Below Standard

If you receive a transcript that does not meet the 99%\+ standard, contact [support@qualtranscribe.com](mailto:support@qualtranscribe.com) within 7 business days of delivery. Include:

- Your order number
- Specific examples of errors with timecodes from the original recording
