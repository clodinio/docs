# Transcription Accuracy

## Human Transcription

Our human transcriptionists deliver high accuracy across a wide range of audio conditions, accents, and subject matters. Accuracy is highest when:

- Audio is clear with minimal background noise
- Speakers are not overlapping
- Recordings are made in a controlled environment

You can improve accuracy further by including notes at checkout — for example, providing speaker names, a list of technical terms, or context about the subject matter.

## Instant Draft

Instant Draft AI accuracy is good for clear, standard recordings but will vary with audio quality. It performs best with:

- Single speaker or clearly separated speakers
- Standard accents and clear enunciation
- Minimal background noise
- General (non-specialist) vocabulary

## Factors that reduce accuracy

The following conditions can reduce transcript accuracy for both services (more so for Instant Draft):

| Factor | Impact |
|---|---|
| Background noise | High |
| Overlapping speakers | High |
| Heavy or unusual accents | Medium–High |
| Domain-specific terminology | Medium |
| Low recording volume | Medium |
| Phone or low-quality microphone | Medium |

## Improving your recordings

Small changes to your recording setup can significantly improve transcript quality:

- Use an external microphone or lapel mic for in-depth interviews
- Position the recorder centrally in focus groups
- Choose a quiet room away from traffic, HVAC systems, and open offices
- Ask participants to speak one at a time where possible
- Record a short test before your full session to check audio levels

<Tip>
  When ordering Human Transcription, use the order notes field to tell your transcriptionist about any unusual terminology, speaker names, or challenging audio sections. This helps them prepare and improves accuracy.
</Tip>
