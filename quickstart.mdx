# Quickstart

This guide takes you from a new account to a completed transcript order. The full process takes under five minutes for most file types.

## Step 1: Create your account

Go to [app.qualtranscribe.com](https://app.qualtranscribe.com) and register with your email address. You will receive a confirmation email with an activation link.

During sign-up, select your **data residency region**. This determines where your files are stored:

- **United States** (us-east-1, North Virginia) for US-based clients and projects
- **European Union** (eu-central-2, Frankfurt) for EU-based clients and GDPR-covered projects

Your data residency region is set at account creation and cannot be changed afterward. If you have institutional, IRB, or legal requirements around data geography, select accordingly before activating your account.

<Note>
  If your organization requires US-only processing with no international data transfer, select United States and note this requirement when placing your first order. See [US-Only Processing](/account/security) for details.
</Note>

## Step 2: Upload your file

**Prepaid accounts:** From your dashboard, click **Order Transcription**. Upload directly from your device or import from a connected cloud service: Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, Box, Zoom, or Webex.

**WorkHub:** Navigate to **WorkHub**, open or create a project folder, and upload your files directly into the workspace.

Supported formats include MP3, MP4, WAV, M4A, and WebM. For very large files (over 2GB), contact support before uploading.

<Tip>
  For best accuracy, record in a quiet environment, position the microphone centrally in multi-speaker sessions, and test your audio quality before submitting a large batch. See [Supported File Formats](/transcription/supported-formats) for full guidance.
</Tip>

## Step 4: Configure your order

For Human Transcription, select your formatting and add-on preferences:

| Option | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Clean Verbatim** | Removes filler words and false starts. Produces readable, professional output. Recommended for most research, legal, and corporate uses. |
| **Full Verbatim** | Captures every utterance including filler words, sighs, repetitions, and non-verbal sounds. Required for discourse analysis, conversation analysis, and legal proceedings where every utterance is evidential. |
| **Timestamps** | Inserts a timestamp at every speaker turn or at 2-minute intervals. Essential for NVivo/ATLAS.ti workflows and legal cross-referencing. |
| **Speaker Labels** | Identifies each participant clearly (e.g., Interviewer, Respondent 1, Moderator, Witness). Critical for focus groups, depositions, and multi-party interviews. |
| **Translation** | Translates the completed transcript into English. Available as bilingual direct transcription (\$6.00/min) or as a two-step process with both source and English versions. |

You can also add order notes to provide context for your transcriptionist: speaker names, technical terminology, subject matter background, or any known audio challenges.

## Step 5: Review and submit

Review your order summary, confirm the turnaround window, and submit payment via Stripe. You will receive an email confirmation with your order reference number.

## Step 6: Receive your transcript

You will receive an email notification when your transcript is ready.

**Human Transcription:** Open your completed transcript in Office Online directly from your account, or download it to your device in your preferred format (DOCX, PDF, SRT, plain text, or timestamped formats).

**Instant Draft:** Open in **Smart Insights** to review, edit, and run AI-powered analysis including thematic mapping, speaker patterns, research memos, and a Coding CSV for qualitative analysis software.

**WorkHub:** Completed transcripts are uploaded directly to your project folder. Your team receives an email notification.

<Check>
  Human Transcription orders come with a free revision round for any confirmed transcription errors. Submit revision requests within 7 business days of delivery with your order number and specific examples.
</Check>
