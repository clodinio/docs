# Transcript De-identification and Redaction

Professional transcript de-identification and redaction services for research, healthcare, and legal transcription. Qualtranscribe removes or replaces Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and Protected Health Information (PHI) from completed transcripts, including names, locations, organizations, and other identifying details.

## The Two Approaches

**De-identification** removes explicit personal identifiers from the transcript, replacing them with neutral placeholders. The resulting transcript cannot be directly linked to a named individual.

Use when: sharing transcripts internally, early-stage research review, internal reporting, or any project where participant identity does not need to be retained.

**Redaction** masks or removes specific identified content from the transcript. Redaction can be applied selectively — removing only the identifiers you specify while leaving the rest of the transcript intact.

Use when: legal proceedings, regulatory submissions, HIPAA compliance workflows, or projects with specific institutional redaction requirements.

## What We Remove or Replace

- Names of people — replaced with `[name]`, or specific where relevant: `[child's name]`, `[sibling]`, `[parent]`
- Cities, states, and countries — replaced with `[city]`, `[state]`, `[country]`
- Businesses or places of work — replaced with `[local business]`, `[restaurant]`, `[shopping center]`
- Hospitals or clinics — replaced with `[hospital]`, `[clinic]`
- Doctors and clinicians — replaced with `[doctor]`, `[therapist]`, `[nurse]`
- Any other identifiers you specify

Drug names, treatment names, and clinical terminology are never redacted unless explicitly requested.

## HIPAA Redaction Format

For clinical and healthcare transcripts, we follow a structured redaction format that includes the identifier type and timestamp for full auditability:

**Format:** `[PHI: descriptor timestamp]`

**Example:**

Original: _"So my daughter Sarah was seen at Boston Children's last Tuesday by Dr. Nguyen, who referred her to the clinic on Commonwealth Ave."_

Redacted: _"So my \[PHI: child's name 00:05:15\] was seen at \[PHI: hospital 00:05:18\] last Tuesday by \[PHI: doctor 00:05:21\], who referred her to the \[PHI: clinic 00:05:25\] on \[PHI: street 00:05:27\]."_

## How to Request

Specify your de-identification or redaction requirements in the **order notes** field when placing your Human Transcription order, or during project discussions with your project manager. Include:

- The approach required (de-identification or redaction)
- Any specific identifiers you know are present
- The replacement format you prefer, or specify HIPAA format if applicable

## HIPAA De-identification Standards

For healthcare research, HIPAA's Safe Harbor method requires removal of all 18 specified PHI identifiers:

| # | Identifier |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Names |
| 2 | Geographic data smaller than state (street address, city, zip code) |
| 3 | Dates (other than year) directly related to an individual |
| 4 | Phone numbers |
| 5 | Fax numbers |
| 6 | Email addresses |
| 7 | Social security numbers |
| 8 | Medical record numbers |
| 9 | Health plan beneficiary numbers |
| 10 | Account numbers |
| 11 | Certificate and license numbers |
| 12 | Vehicle identifiers and serial numbers |
| 13 | Device identifiers and serial numbers |
| 14 | Web URLs |
| 15 | IP addresses |
| 16 | Biometric identifiers (finger and voice prints) |
| 17 | Full-face photographs and comparable images |
| 18 | Any other unique identifying number or code |

Qualtranscribe applies Safe Harbor de-identification by default for healthcare transcripts unless you specify otherwise.

## PII Redaction for Instant Draft (AI Transcription)

Instant Draft includes a built-in PII Redaction feature that automatically detects and redacts personally identifiable information directly from your transcript during processing. To enable it, select **Anonymize** in the Instant Draft options at checkout.

### How It Works

When PII Redaction is enabled, identified personal information is replaced in the transcript using one of two substitution methods:

**Hash substitution** replaces PII with `####`:

> _"Hi, my name is ####!"_

**Entity name substitution** replaces PII with a labeled placeholder:

> _"Hi, my name is \[PERSON\_NAME\]!"_

Select your preferred substitution method when enabling the feature.
