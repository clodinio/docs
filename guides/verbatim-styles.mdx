---

## Clean Verbatim

Clean Verbatim (also called Intelligent Verbatim) removes filler words, false starts, and verbal repetitions while preserving the complete meaning and context of what was said.

What is removed:

- Filler words: um, uh, like, you know, sort of, kind of
- False starts: "I was going to — what I mean is..."
- Repetitions: "I think, I think what we need to do..."
- Non-verbal sounds that do not carry meaning

What is kept:

- Everything the speaker intended to say
- Grammatical constructions as spoken (not corrected to formal grammar)
- Interruptions and incomplete sentences where they carry meaning
- Laughter and emotionally significant non-verbal cues (optional)

**Use Clean Verbatim for:**

- Most research interviews and focus groups
- Market research, UX research, and usability studies
- Academic qualitative research focused on content and theme
- Legal advisory summaries and internal documentation
- Corporate meetings and stakeholder interviews
- Any project where readability and efficient coding matter more than speech pattern analysis

## Full Verbatim

Full Verbatim captures every utterance exactly as spoken. Nothing is removed.

What is included:

- All filler words: every um, uh, like, you know
- All false starts and self-corrections
- All repetitions
- Sighs, laughter, pauses, throat-clearing
- Non-verbal sounds: \[laughs\], \[sighs\], \[long pause\]
- Incomplete sentences and interrupted thoughts exactly as spoken

**Use Full Verbatim for:**

- Discourse analysis and conversation analysis
- Linguistic research where speech patterns are data
- Legal depositions where every utterance is part of the record
- Behavioral health research where hesitation, self-correction, and emotional markers are analytically significant
- Psychotherapy session documentation
- Any project where how something is said matters as much as what was said

Example of the same passage in each style:

**Audio:** "Um, I, I think it's, uh, kind of complicated, you know? Like, when you really, really look at it..."

**Full Verbatim:** "Um, I, I think it's, uh, kind of complicated, you know? Like, when you really, really look at it..."

**Clean Verbatim:** "I think it's complicated. When you really look at it..."

## Hybrid Style

A hybrid approach removes filler words but retains significant non-verbal cues (laughter, long pauses, emotional breaks, notable silences).

**Use the hybrid style for:**

- Focus groups where emotional reactions and group dynamics are part of the analysis
- Personal interviews where tone is relevant but filler-heavy transcripts reduce readability
- Research involving sensitive topics where emotional markers matter
- Oral history recordings where rhythm and affect are part of the record

To request the hybrid style, specify in your order notes: "Clean Verbatim, retaining \[laughter\], \[long pause\], and \[emotional cues\]."

## Which Style for Which Use Case

| Use Case | Recommended Style |
| --- | --- |
| Academic research interviews | Clean Verbatim |
| Discourse or conversation analysis | Full Verbatim |
| Legal depositions | Full Verbatim |
| Focus groups (market research) | Clean Verbatim or Hybrid |
| UX research and usability tests | Clean Verbatim |
| Clinical patient interviews | Clean Verbatim or Full (if behavioral) |
| Oral history projects | Clean Verbatim or Hybrid |
| Corporate meetings | Clean Verbatim |
| Behavioral health research | Full Verbatim |
| Journalism and content | Clean Verbatim |

## Cost Difference

Full Verbatim takes longer to transcribe because every utterance must be captured precisely. This typically adds 20-30% to the base transcription time and cost. If you are unsure which style you need, consider starting with Clean Verbatim; if you discover that speech patterns are analytically significant once you begin your analysis, you can request a Full Verbatim revision for specific interviews.

## After You Receive Your Transcript

Verbatim style cannot easily be changed after delivery. Adding Full Verbatim elements to a Clean Verbatim transcript requires re-transcription. Changing the style after ordering rather than specifying it upfront may incur additional fees. Specify your style clearly when placing your order.
